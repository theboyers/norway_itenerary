Driving

Rental car for the full trip (except Route 5 which uses train + flight + rental in Lofoten). Most drives are 2–5.5 hours through scenic mountain and fjord roads. Toll roads are common - AutoPASS covers all tolls automatically. Ferries are part of the normal road network. Norwegian Scenic Routes (Sognefjellet, Aurlandsfjellet, Valdresflye, Trollstigen) are highlights of many routes.

Weather & Daylight

Late May / early June: 10–20°C (50–68°F) in valleys/fjords; 5–15°C (40–60°F) in mountains and Lofoten. Sunset ~10:30 PM – midnight depending on latitude, sunrise ~4:00 AM. Lofoten has 24-hour daylight starting May 27. Rain gear essential - fjord weather changes fast. Glacier areas stay cooler. Pack layers: base layer, fleece, waterproof outer.

Jet Lag Playbook

The problem: Depart 6pm EDT → Arrive 7am CEST (8h flight). Your body thinks it's 1am - you have to survive a full Oslo day.

Before the flight: Shift meals and wake time 1h earlier for 2–3 days prior. Hydrate hard. No alcohol on the plane.

On the plane: Set watch to CEST immediately. Eat dinner onboard, then sleep 4–5 hours. Eye mask, earplugs, neck pillow. Wake up 90 min before landing - drink water, skip the coffee.

Arrival (7am CEST): Your room won't be ready. Drop bags at the hotel, then go outside immediately. Natural light is the best reset. Eat a real breakfast (protein, not just pastries). No napping. If you absolutely must, set an alarm for 20 min max.

Survive Day 1: Walk the city (Aker Brygge, Opera House, Akershus Fortress). Movement beats lethargy. Caffeine before noon only. Stay awake until 9pm local time at minimum. Take a melatonin-3mg at 8pm if you're fading.

Language

Norwegian is the official language, but English is widely spoken everywhere. Road signs use Nynorsk/Bokmål - same alphabet as English. Learning a few words (takk = thank you, hei = hello, ha det = goodbye) is appreciated.

Connectivity

Unlimited data + hotspot for everyone. Norway has excellent 4G/5G coverage in towns and along main roads. Remote fjord and mountain areas may have spotty service. Two approaches:

eSIM (easiest): Each person installs an Airalo or Holafly Norway eSIM before departure. Unlimited data for 10 days ~$20–40. Supports hotspot tethering. No physical SIM swap - install on both iPhone and Android.

Physical SIM: Pick up a Telia or Telenor prepaid SIM at Oslo Airport arrivals. Unlimited data ~$30–50 for 30 days. Hotspot included. One SIM per car can share Wi-Fi to everyone in the vehicle.

Group plan: Each family gets one data plan and enables hotspot. The three travelers in each car share one connection. Download offline Google Maps for the entire route as backup. Remote areas (Lærdal tunnel, Jotunheimen valleys, Lofoten mountain passes) may have no signal regardless.

Travel Insurance

"Cancel for Any Reason" (CFAR) - Both families should book CFAR policies. Covers 50–75% of prepaid trip costs if you cancel for any reason not listed in standard coverage. Must be purchased within 14–21 days of first trip deposit.

Medical & evacuation: Standard travel insurance covers medical emergencies abroad ($100k–500k), emergency dental, and medical evacuation to nearest adequate facility. Confirm your plan covers Norway (Schengen area). Some credit cards (Chase Sapphire Preferred/Reserve, Capital One Venture X) include travel medical - verify limits before buying separate.

Other protection: Trip delay (6+ hour delays → $500–2,000 reimbursement), baggage loss/delay, rental car collision (verify if your credit card covers CDW in Norway), and adventure sports coverage if doing Via Ferrata, glacier hiking, or whitewater rafting (some policies exclude "hazardous activities").

Recommendations: Allianz (OneTrip Premier), Travel Guard (Preferred), World Nomads (adventure-friendly). Expect ~5–10% of total trip cost for CFAR policies.

When to Book

Flights (PIT/MGW → various Norwegian airports): Book 3–5 months out (Jan–Feb 2027). Best prices seen Tue–Wed. Use Google Flights price tracking. From Pittsburgh (PIT), connecting through JFK/EWR or direct to a European hub (LHR, CDG, FRA) then connect. Aim for Economy Plus ~$800–1,200 round trip. Open-jaw itineraries (fly into one city, out of another) are common.

Car rental: Book 8–12 weeks ahead (Mar–early Apr 2027). Use Discover Cars or RentalCars.com. Mid-size SUV or station wagon recommended. Expect ~$400–800 total per car. Check for free cancellation.

Hotels: Book 4–6 months ahead (Dec 2026–Jan 2027). Popular properties (Fretheim, Alexandra, Lofoten rorbuer) sell out first - book even earlier for Lofoten (6+ months). Use Booking.com with free cancellation, rebook if rates drop.

Tip: Set calendar reminders for each booking window. Check hotel websites directly for member rates.

  • Passports: All passports valid & expire at least 6 months after trip (Nov 2027)
  • Passport copies: Scan all passports - save to phone + leave paper copy at home
  • International Driving Permit (IDP): Apply at AAA - $30+ per driver. Not legally required in Norway, but rental car companies may require it and it helps with police/traffic stops

  • Print confirmations: Flights, hotels, car rental, activity bookings (paper backup)
  • Hotel loyalty numbers: Add to Booking.com / hotel accounts before booking
  • Emergency contact list: Each family saves each other's flight info, hotel addresses, phone numbers

  • Research ferry routes & schedules - book in advance where possible

Financial 0/6

  • Notify banks of travel dates (May 28 – Jun 6) - avoid fraud blocks
  • Check foreign transaction fees - use cards with 0% FTF (Chase Sapphire, Capital One, etc.)
  • Set/confirm PIN for all credit cards - Norway uses chip + PIN everywhere
  • Get Norwegian kroner (NOK) - ~$200–300 cash per family for small purchases
  • Check ATM withdrawal limits - increase daily limit if needed
  • Add Apple Pay / Google Pay - widely accepted in Norway

Packing 0/12

  • Layers - base layer (merino), fleece/mid layer, waterproof outer

  • Power adapter - European Type C/F, 230V (US devices need converter for high-wattage)
  • Quick-dry towel - for glacier hikes, kayaking, or spontaneous swims
  • Reusable water bottle - Norway tap water is excellent everywhere
  • Daypack - 20–30L for hikes and day trips
  • Sunglasses + sunscreen - long daylight hours, glacier glare is intense
  • Swimwear - hotel pools, hot tubs, saunas, fjord dips
  • Binoculars - fjord cruises, wildlife (seals, eagles, reindeer)
  • Dry bags - for kayaking, boat cruises, wet gear separation
  • Travel laundry kit - sink stopper, travel soap, clothesline
